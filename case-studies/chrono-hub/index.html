How Chrono Hub Built a $10M+ Pipeline From Zero in 18 Months

In 18 months, Acquiro helped Chrono Hub go from no audience, no data, and no presence to a $10M+ pipeline of qualified inbound enquiries, multi-million dollars in client profit, and a content engine that gets stronger every month. All of it built on around $190K of ad spend in one of the world's most competitive luxury markets.

Chrono Hub is a luxury watch, designer handbag, jewellery, and art studio based in Dubai. They sit at the very top of the high-ticket retail market, with individual pieces selling for anywhere from five figures to half a million dollars, and they sell to a clientele where trust matters more than price and the buying cycle runs in weeks and months rather than days.

When Chrono Hub came to us, they had a brand new physical location, an exceptional in-store experience, and access to some of the most coveted inventory in the region. What they didn't have was an audience.

No customer base. No email list. No social following. No pixel data. No website traffic worth retargeting. A complete cold start in one of the most saturated, trust-dependent categories on earth.

The brief covered everything. Build the audience, generate awareness, nurture inbound interest, and convert it. Top of funnel to closed deal.

Challenge: Building demand in a category that runs on trust

When we mapped the situation against the realities of luxury retail in the Middle East, three things shaped everything that came after.

  1. A complete cold start in a long-cycle category. With no first-party data, the algorithm had nothing to learn from. In a vertical where buying cycles run weeks to months and average order values sit in the five and six figures, you can't shortcut the trust layer with aggressive direct response. The audience had to be built and warmed before any conversion mechanic was going to fire.
  2. Middle Eastern commerce doesn't behave like the rest of the world. A huge proportion of high-ticket buying decisions in the region happen inside WhatsApp and Instagram DMs, not on websites. A standard checkout-driven funnel would leave the majority of qualified intent invisible. The funnel had to be designed around conversations, not clicks.
  3. The product is the experience as much as the inventory. Chrono Hub's positioning isn't only about what they sell. It's about the physical space, the service, the way they treat customers, and the rarity of the in-person experience. None of that translates through a static product ad, so every layer of what we built had to carry the experience as well as the piece.

Chrono Hub didn't need a campaign. They needed a system designed for how luxury actually sells in this region.

Process: A three-tier funnel, built for the long cycle

We built an acquisition system across three layers that fed one another, with content as the fuel and paid media as the amplifier.

1. Foundation

The first few weeks went into defining who Chrono Hub was actually for. Not a generic ICP, but a specific picture of the kind of buyer who walks into a Dubai luxury watch studio with serious intent: collectors, gifting buyers, returning customers, and high-net-worth individuals relocating to the UAE.

Alongside that we installed the full tracking layer. Meta Pixel, custom audiences for video viewers, profile engagers, message starters, website visitors. Every meaningful behaviour on every platform got saved as an audience we could retarget against later.

We also locked the principle that drove everything from day one: content first, paid amplification on top, native to the social platforms. The goal was to generate conversations on Instagram and WhatsApp, not push people onto a checkout page they weren't ready for.

2. Engine

Once the foundations were in, the three-tier funnel went live.

Top of funnel. Awareness content amplified with paid media. The job here wasn't to sell. It was to introduce Chrono Hub to the right audiences, drive follows, build familiarity, and position the studio itself as a destination worth visiting. Volume mattered. Over 50 pieces of high-grade content per month were going through this layer organically and through ads, which gave us creative data quickly and let us see what was actually landing.

Middle and bottom of funnel. Direct-response campaigns built around two conversion actions. Either a DM conversation on Instagram or WhatsApp, or a specific enquiry on the website about a piece. These campaigns retargeted everyone who had engaged with the top of funnel content, and tested cold audiences in parallel. ManyChat automations triggered on Instagram story replies and certain keywords, capturing intent inside the platform without forcing people off it.

Re-engagement and retention. Every audience that interacted at any layer was saved and cycled back into top of funnel content. Existing customers were retargeted with new inventory drops to build repeat purchases. Most agencies skip this part. In luxury retail it's the layer that does the heaviest lifting over time, because the average lifetime value of a customer in this category is enormous and word of mouth in the Middle Eastern luxury market travels further than anywhere else we've worked.

3. The flywheel

By around month six the system stopped needing us to push it. New content fed the top of funnel, the top of funnel fed the middle, the middle fed conversations, conversations fed sales, and every sale fed back into retention loops.

We also worked directly with Chrono Hub's team on speed to lead. In luxury, replying inside the first ten minutes is worth multiples of replying three hours later, so the architecture only does its job if the human handoff at the end of it is sharp. That side of things mattered as much as anything we built in the ad account.

Outcome: 18 months of compounding

Over 18 months and around $190K (roughly 700,000 AED) in ad spend, the system has produced:

  • $10M+ (37M+ AED) in qualified pipeline value
  • Multi-million dollars in client profit generated from that pipeline
  • Around 10× return on ad spend in profit terms
  • A consistent monthly inbound lead pipeline that didn't exist before we started
  • An established audience and content engine in a category where most brands take three to five years to build presence

The bigger shift is that Chrono Hub now runs on a system rather than effort. New audiences come in, existing ones get nurtured, customers buy again, and the work compounds month over month instead of resetting.

The next phase, already underway, is back-end streamlining. We're rolling out Respond.io and a set of pre-qualification automations that will take more friction out of the sales team's day and unlock another step-change in pipeline velocity.

Lessons: What luxury actually rewards

A few things became clearer than ever working on Chrono Hub.

Luxury rewards trust, not urgency. Every funnel mechanic in this category has to honour that. Scarcity tactics, countdown timers, and aggressive direct response don't work on a buyer who's spending $200K on a watch. The system has to earn the conversation before it can ask for anything.

In the Middle East, the conversation is the funnel. Designing for DMs and WhatsApp isn't a tactic, it's the requirement. Any funnel that tries to force website checkouts in this market is leaving the majority of intent invisible.

Pipeline value beats cost per lead. Optimising for a cheap CPL in a long-cycle, high-AOV category gets you a cheap list of unqualified people. We optimised for the value of the pipeline the system was building, not the cost of any individual click. That's the only number that matters in this category.

Final thoughts

Chrono Hub didn't have a marketing problem when they came to us. They had an infrastructure gap. A brand new luxury studio in a saturated market with no audience to talk to and no system to bring one to them.

Eighteen months later they have both, and the pipeline keeps growing.
