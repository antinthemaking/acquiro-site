How Nomadic Outdoors Generated $116K in Four Months on Under $9K of Ad Spend

Across the 2025/2026 camping season, Acquiro helped Nomadic Outdoors go from an organic-only business to $116,000 (427,000 AED) in direct booking revenue, with average order value almost doubling and a multi-six-figure event pipeline already lined up for the next season. The whole thing ran on under $9,000 of ad spend across 16 weeks.

Nomadic Outdoors is a camping rental and outdoor equipment business operating across the Middle East, with a parallel arm running organised tours, trips, and outdoor events. The camping season is the window where everything has to perform. Every week that doesn't convert is revenue the business can't recover later in the year.

Heading into the new season, Nomadic was in a familiar position. The business was growing on the back of strong organic content, an engaged Instagram and TikTok audience, and consistent traffic to the website. But organic alone wasn't going to carry them through peak season, and there was nothing in place to turn that organic momentum into something that scaled.

That's the brief we picked up.

Challenge: A seasonal business with no system underneath it

When we looked at Nomadic's setup heading into the season, three things shaped the work.

  1. A finite window with no margin for waste. Seasonal businesses don't get second chances. The camping season runs for a fixed number of months and every week that doesn't convert is revenue the business will never recover. The infrastructure had to be built before the season started, not during it, and every layer had to be ready to scale from day one.
  2. No system behind the organic momentum. Nomadic had a sizable audience on Instagram and TikTok, consistent organic website traffic, and a strong content output. None of it was being retargeted, re-engaged, or amplified through paid media. The attention was there, the audience was there, but nothing was turning either of them into bookings outside of whoever happened to message the sales team that day.
  3. Bookings ran through humans, with all the friction that comes with it. Every enquiry hit the sales team. The same questions came in over and over. The team was spending most of its time answering basic information requests instead of closing larger bookings or working on the events pipeline. Customers who weren't ready to talk to a human were dropping off before they ever became a booking.

What Nomadic needed wasn't more attention. They had plenty of that. What they needed was the system that turned the attention they already had into bookings without losing customers to friction along the way.

Process: Build the infrastructure, then run paid media on top of it

The approach was infrastructure first. Get the foundations right, then scale with paid media on top of something that was actually ready to convert.

1. Foundation

The biggest single piece of the foundation was a fully bespoke booking platform, built from scratch in Claude Code and designed around Nomadic's exact operating model. Nomadic had tried Bokun, Logify, and other off-the-shelf rental platforms before. None of them fit. The booking parameters, the equipment configurations, the pricing logic, the upsell flow. Every detail of how Nomadic actually ran needed a custom build, and no template platform was going to deliver it.

With the platform live, customers could book directly without ever needing to speak to a human, with full visibility of equipment, dates, and pricing.

Alongside that we rolled out Respond.io with a layer of automations designed to take the sales team out of repetitive enquiry handling. The same questions that customers had been asking the team over and over were now answered automatically, with the right information delivered at the right moment, and a clean handoff into the booking platform when a customer was ready to commit. Time saved for the sales team across the season was roughly 80%, which freed them up to work on larger bookings and the events pipeline instead.

The final piece was the tracking layer. Server-side tracking through the Meta Conversions API, full custom audience builds across video viewers, profile engagers, website visitors, and existing customers, and proper attribution across every channel that mattered.

2. Engine

Once the foundations were in, we layered paid media on top across the Infinite Funnel sequence.

Re-engagement first. The fastest revenue in the system was always going to come from the audience Nomadic had already built. We launched re-engagement and retargeting campaigns first, hitting the organic Instagram and TikTok audiences, existing website traffic, and past customers. Nomadic's content is genuinely visually rich and performs natively on the platforms, which gave us creative we could amplify straight away.

Cold traffic next. Once re-engagement was firing, we layered cold acquisition campaigns on top across Meta and Google. We also tested YouTube Shorts as a direct-response channel. It performed well enough out of the gate that scaling it has become part of the plan for the next season.

Lifetime value layer. With the booking platform handling direct bookings, we ran a parallel set of campaigns aimed at increasing average order value. Volume booking promotions, multi-equipment bundles, and offers structured around how customers actually rented. AOV moved from around $355 (1,300 AED) per booking to between $625 and $650 (2,300 to 2,400 AED) across the season. Close to a doubling on the same audience.

3. Optimisation

With direct-response data flowing in from the booking platform, the campaigns optimised week over week. Top-performing creatives got more spend, underperformers got cut, and creative iteration ran continuously through the season.

By the end of the 16-week window, the system was producing consistent direct bookings, the sales team was off low-value enquiry work, and a pipeline of larger-scale events for the next season was being built in parallel.

Outcome: A six-figure season on a five-figure spend

Across the 16 weeks of the 2025/2026 camping season (October 2025 to February 2026):

  • $116,000 (427,000 AED) in direct booking revenue
  • Under $9,000 in total ad spend across the season
  • Approximately 13× return on ad spend
  • Average order value nearly doubled, from around $355 to $625 to $650 (1,300 AED to 2,300 to 2,400 AED) per booking
  • A multi-six-figure event pipeline for the 2026/2027 season already lined up alongside the rental business

The bigger picture is that Nomadic now goes into the next season with a proven acquisition system, a custom booking platform that runs without manual intervention, and the audience data to scale much harder in the next window than was possible in this one.

Lessons: What seasonal businesses actually need

A few things became clearer than ever working on Nomadic.

Build the infrastructure before the season starts, not during it. For seasonal operators, the calendar is the constraint. The system has to be live and proven from day one of the window, which means the foundation work has to happen in the off-season. Trying to build the booking platform while the season is running is how you waste the only weeks that actually matter.

Use the organic audience you already have. A lot of operators with strong organic content never run a single retargeting campaign against that audience. That's the cheapest revenue any business will ever generate, and it's sitting there waiting to be turned into bookings. The system to convert organic momentum should be in place before anyone spends a dollar on cold traffic.

Off-the-shelf doesn't always fit. Nomadic had cycled through Bokun, Logify, and other rental platforms before we got involved. None of them worked for how the business actually ran. The custom booking platform was a bigger build up front, but it paid for itself inside the first few weeks of the season and continues to scale cleanly into the next one.

Final thoughts

Nomadic came into the season with strong content, an engaged audience, and a sales team doing the best they could with no system underneath them. Sixteen weeks later they had $116K (427,000 AED) in direct revenue, a custom booking platform running their bookings without manual intervention, and a pipeline already lined up for the next season.

The system is built. The audience is warm. The next window opens with everything ready to run.
